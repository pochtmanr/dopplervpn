Which Device Goes Which Way?

As the user scrolls down, one device drifts up and the other drifts down

↑
↓
Android ↓
iPhone ↑

A. iPhone Up, Android Down

iPhone (front, primary) rises to the top. Android (behind) sinks to the bottom. Emphasizes iOS as the hero product — natural since iPhone is in front and "above."

↑
↓
Android ↑
iPhone ↓

B. Android Up, iPhone Down

Android rises, iPhone sinks. Creates a crossing/diverging motion since iPhone starts in front-lower and Android starts behind-upper. More dynamic visual tension.

Note: Both options start with devices overlapping in the center. As the user scrolls, they drift apart toward the top/bottom edges of the hero section. The effect is subtle at first and intensifies with scroll depth.
